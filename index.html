Executive Summary: Key Performance Insights

Precision Crisis

6.5% precision rate means 93.5% of flagged content is not actually violating policy. This creates massive review overhead and may impact user trust.

Confidence Miscalibration

The model is most confident on false positives (0.8+) but actual violations cluster in mid-range (0.5-0.7). Confidence scores cannot be trusted.

Systematic Over-flagging

All categories show similar poor performance (~2% precision), indicating a fundamental model issue rather than category-specific problems.

⚠️ Recommendation: Do Not Scale
Given the 6.5% precision rate and 47.7% false positive rate, this model requires significant improvement before production deployment. Consider: (1) comprehensive retraining with corrected labels, (2) raising confidence thresholds to 0.7+, (3) implementing mandatory human review for all flags, or (4) exploring alternative models/approaches.
